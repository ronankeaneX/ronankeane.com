# Voice and style

## Audience first

SMB and mid-market B2B leaders ÔÇö CEOs, Presidents, Founders, COOs, CROs, CMOs, VPs of Operations. They are skeptical of hype, focused on measurable ROI, and read at an executive level. **Never frame content as "enterprise."**

## Voice

- **Direct.** Lead with the point.
- **Confident, not boastful.** State the claim and back it with evidence.
- **Second-person.** "You", "your team", "your initiative."
- **Concrete over abstract.** Names, numbers, specific tools, specific outcomes.
- **Short sentences.** Paragraphs of 2-4 sentences max.

## Forbidden phrases

Never use:
- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "synergy" / "synergies"
- "unlock potential"
- "revolutionary"
- "game-changer", "game-changing"
- "in today's fast-paced world", "in the digital age"
- "cutting-edge"
- "world-class"
- "next-generation"
- "best-in-class"
- "enterprise" (as audience framing ÔÇö Ronan serves SMB/mid-market)

## Numbers and statistics

- Always specific: `$275M`, `70%`, `5% Ôćĺ 65%`, `200+ users`. Never "significant", "substantial", "major".
- Always attributed: name the source or context (e.g., "GTT Communications, 2022-2024 engagement").
- Use the proof figures consistently ÔÇö they appear across the site and reinforce each other.

## CTAs

- Primary, everywhere: **"Schedule a consultation"** Ôćĺ `https://calendly.com/ronankeane/ai-revenue-acceleration-readiness-discovery-call`
- Secondary, everywhere: **"Send a message"** Ôćĺ `/contact/`
- Optional micro-meta below CTAs: `"~ 20 min ┬Ě no pitch deck"` (sets expectation, signals seniority)

## Framework references

- It's always **PRIME** (Potential Mapping, Roadmap & Strategy, Implementation Planning, Migration & Execution, Enablement & Adoption)
- Never PRISMA. The old WordPress site has this typo in 3 case studies ÔÇö never repeat it.
- When referencing the framework on a content page, link to `/prime-framework/`.

## Year references

The current year is acceptable to reference naturally ("As of 2026...") ÔÇö it helps freshness signals. Don't over-do it.
