## What an AI Consultant Actually Does for Your Business

An AI consultant doesn't just recommend software. The work falls into three areas: strategy, training, and implementation.

### AI Strategy & Roadmap

I start by mapping where AI can create the most value in your business — not in the abstract, but in your specific workflows, team structure, and competitive context.

Together, we build a phased implementation plan that balances quick wins with long-term transformation. You get clear recommendations on tools, training needs, budget requirements, and expected ROI for each initiative.

The output is a concrete roadmap, not a 60-page PowerPoint deck that sits in a drawer.

### AI Training & Workshops

Your team learns AI skills through hands-on workshops customized to their roles. This isn't death by PowerPoint. It's practical training where people bring their actual work and learn to solve real problems with AI.

Workshop topics include:

- ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini mastery for business users
- Prompt engineering for real business tasks (not toy examples)
- AI-assisted research, writing, and analysis
- Identifying which tasks AI should and shouldn't handle
- Understanding AI risk: hallucinations, bias, and brand exposure

Proper change management increases adoption rates by 70%. Teams that receive contextual, role-specific training actually use the tools — instead of letting expensive software collect dust.

### AI Automation & Implementation

I design and implement AI-powered workflows that integrate into your existing systems. Using tools like n8n and leading AI APIs, I build automations that handle repetitive tasks, process documents, enrich data, and more.

Every implementation includes documentation and team handoff so your people can maintain and extend what we build together.

### AI SEO / Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)

As of 2026, search behavior is shifting. Buyers are getting answers from ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity before they ever reach your website. This emerging field — Generative Engine Optimization — ensures your brand maintains visibility as that shift accelerates.

I audit how AI models currently represent your business and implement structured content strategies that make you the answer AI tools recommend.

When companies implement AI through IT or a tech vendor, the focus is on the tool: "Is it installed? Is it secure? Is it running?" That's necessary, but it's not sufficient.

## How We Work Together

### Phase 1: Audit & Discovery

We map your current processes, identify the highest-impact AI opportunities, and assess your team's readiness. This phase produces a clear picture of where AI can move the needle fastest.

An **AI Readiness Assessment** evaluates your organization's current capabilities, data infrastructure, and cultural preparedness for AI adoption. It identifies gaps in technology, skills, and processes before you invest — preventing costly missteps and ensuring your foundation supports successful implementation.

### Phase 2: Strategy & Roadmap

We build a prioritized, phased implementation plan based on business impact, resource requirements, and dependencies. You get clear recommendations on tools, training needs, budget, and expected ROI at each milestone.

A good **AI Strategy Roadmap** prevents "shiny object syndrome" and ensures AI investments align with business goals — delivering quick wins while building toward long-term transformation.

### Phase 3: Training & Enablement

Your team gets hands-on, role-specific training before any new tool goes live. We focus on practical application: real tasks, real prompts, real results. People who understand why a tool helps them adopt it. People who get a technical demo do not.

### Phase 4: Implementation & Optimization

We build and deploy the AI solutions identified in your roadmap — automation workflows, integrated tools, and custom implementations. Each build is scoped as a **Proof of Concept (POC)** first: a small-scale, time-boxed project that validates whether an AI solution can solve a specific business problem before full deployment. POCs reduce risk and build stakeholder confidence by demonstrating real value with actual data.

Successful pilots are then scaled across the organization with continuous monitoring and performance optimization.
